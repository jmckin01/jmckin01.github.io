Research

I am interested in studying the formation and evolution of galaxies, through the lens of mid- and far-Infrared observations. Ten billion years ago, the star formation rate density of the Universe peaked and individual galaxies were forming more stars than at any other time in cosmic history. At this so-called "cosmic noon", galaxies host larger gas reservoirs, and are more efficient at forming stars. The physical basis for higher star-formation efficiencies in the past remains a key open question in galaxy evolution, and the answer lies in the details of energy transfer in galaxies. To better understand these processes, my research focuses on characterizing the physical conditions of star-forming gas in the most actively star-forming galaxies in the local Universe and at cosmic noon. As an observational astronomer, I use a wide range of facilities including ALMA, HST, Spitzer, the VLA, and Keck. Click here for a copy of my CV, and here for a complete list of my publications.
